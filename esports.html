History of South Korean Esports

Esports in South Korea has a long history, beginning in the early 2000s when competitive gaming gained immense popularity. Initially driven by the game "StarCraft," esports tournaments started drawing large audiences, leading to the emergence of professional players and teams. South Korea's supportive gaming culture, along with the rise of new titles like "League of Legends" and "Overwatch," solidified the country's reputation as a global esports powerhouse, with fans cheering for their favorite teams and players in various exciting competitions. Today, esports has become an integral part of South Korean youth culture, with players achieving celebrity status and tournaments attracting widespread attention.

Esports Venues and PC Cafes

Seoul E-Stadium

Located in Gangnam, Seoul E-Stadium is a popular venue for various esports events. It has hosted tournaments for games like StarCraft II and League of Legends.

LoL Park

Dedicated to League of Legends (LoL) esports, LoL Park is located in Jongno-gu, Seoul. It serves as the home stadium for the League of Legends Champions Korea (LCK) league.

PC Bang

PC Bang is a chain of PC gaming cafes with locations throughout South Korea. It's one of the most widespread and recognizable names in the industry.

Nexon Arena Cafe

Nexon Arena is a gaming venue and PC cafe associated with Nexon, a major South Korean video game developer. It provides a unique experience, combining esports events with a cafe setting.
